## Initiative Mid-Semester Report

**Title:** The Lab-to-Venture (L2V) Framework: A Train-the-Trainer Commercialization Pipeline

**Describe your initiative/ Procedure.**
This initiative proposes a structured training procedure targeted at the management class within the research group. The goal is to provide a standardized framework that empowers current and future managers to train researchers on translating their academic work into commercial pitch decks, identifying industry partners, and sustaining those relationships long-term. By shifting to a decentralized, train-the-trainer model, the initiative aims to build a clear "on-ramp" for researchers to utilize their own networks and scale the organization's commercialization efforts.

**Explain the hypotheses/ KPIs you have measured at this time and what is left to be measured.**
* **Hypothesis:** If the management class is equipped with a standardized "train-the-trainer" commercialization framework, they can effectively instruct researchers on how to build compelling pitch decks, leverage existing networks, and independently sustain external partnerships. This decentralized approach will eliminate administrative bottlenecks and prevent relationship decay caused by semesterly management turnover.
* **KPIs:**
    * **Sustainment Rate:** Tracking the percentage of partnerships that survive academic semester transitions without becoming disorganized. *(To be measured at semester end).*
    * **Network Utilization:** Measuring the number of new industry partners successfully recruited through researchers' internal networks via the new "on-ramp". *(Currently tracking).*
    * **Event Execution:** Successfully hosting Pitch Day and gathering feedback from VCs on the researchers' self-delivered pitches. *(Left to be measured).*

**Explain how stakeholders are engaging with your initiative. Reflect on whether their engagement matches your expectations and what changes may be necessary given the behavior that you observed.**
* **Primary (Management Class):** Expected to use this procedure to train others. A critical required change in behavior is ensuring managers explicitly step back from direct, day-to-day communication with external partners to decentralize the relationship.
* **Secondary (Researchers):** Expected to receive training to build pitch decks and sustain relationships. A major behavioral shift observed is the necessity for researchers to break the habit of "academic presenting" and learn to deliver a concise, commercially viable narrative. 
* **Tertiary (External Partners/VCs):** Expected to engage in streamlined, researcher-led collaborations. 

**What processes have you documented or begun documenting to ensure the sustainability of your initiative provide where you are hosting this procedure? What additional documentation do you plan to complete? Link documents here for review.**
* **Currently Documented:**

    * **Appendix B: The "Pitch Day" Readiness Curriculum & Event Logistics:** Outlines the curriculum for live investor presentations and the operational guidelines for hosting a centralized Pitch Day.

**How are you currently measuring progress toward your goals? What indicators of success or challenges have you identified so far?**
* Progress is measured through the completion of 1-on-1 "Network Audit" sessions and the execution of "Mock Delivery" practice runs.
* **Indicators of Success:** Researchers effectively mastering "PM" elements by clearly articulating their project's strategic positioning and competitive differentiation.
* **Challenges:** Ensuring researchers adapt to the "Bottom Line Up Front" (BLUF) presentation style rather than building up to a conclusion, which is the academic standard.

**What obstacles or bottlenecks have you encountered in implementing your initiative? Which anticipated challenges have materialized, and what unexpected issues have arisen?**
* **Anticipated Challenges:** Flipping the researchers' presentation style from academic to commercial has required significant coaching. Enforcing the "Scope Check" to ensure potential external collaborations do not alter the fundamental scope of the project, keeping faculty as the sole driver.
* **Unexpected Issues:** Breaking the habit of management stepping in to handle email follow-ups. We had to strictly enforce that the management class does not send follow-up emails to interested VCs, requiring managers to actively step out of the communication loop.
