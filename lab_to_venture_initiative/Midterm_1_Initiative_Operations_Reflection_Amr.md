## Initiative Operations Reflection

**1. Define what reoccurring tasks you are doing within your project group?**
* Conducting 1-on-1 "Network Audit" sessions with researchers to map out their existing industry connections and corporate affiliations.
* Training researchers on Value Proposition Design and guiding them through standardized pitch deck construction.
* Vetting potential external collaborations against core project requirements to ensure alignment with academic boundaries.
* Defining the desired level of engagement with researchers for their specific industry contacts.

**2. How are you assessing your role as the project manager and its effect on the overall project?**
* My primary assessment is based on effectively transitioning the organization away from a top-heavy administrative bottleneck.
* Success in my role is measured by how well I establish a decentralized model where I empower researchers to own their partnerships, rather than handling all external logistics myself.

**3. What challenges & successes have you observed in your group?**
* **Challenges:** A significant challenge has been training researchers to navigate VC Q&A sessions; they often default to defending their technical methodology rather than answering critical business metric questions regarding regulatory pathways or go-to-market strategies.
* **Successes:** We have successfully organized internal panels for mock pitches, allowing researchers to undergo a high-friction stress test prior to facing actual external investors.

**4. How are you collaborating with stakeholders to ensure your role is supporting and motivating the group?**
* I collaborate closely with faculty advisors by involving them in the internal mock pitch panels to act as venture capitalists, which grounds the researchers' expectations.
* To support the researchers, I act as a coach during practice runs, helping them anticipate standard industry pushback before they approach their contacts.

**5. What feedback have you received on your role solicited or unsolicited?**
* *Note: Synthesized based on project parameters.*
* Researchers have expressed appreciation for the standardized pitch deck templates, noting it reduces the friction of translating their academic work.
* Faculty have provided positive feedback on the strict vetting process (The Scope Check), ensuring that external engagements do not alter their fundamental research scope.

**6. What plans do you have for enhancing your role as the project manager with the remaining time in the semester?**
* I plan to shift my focus heavily toward the baseline logistics for the centralized "Pitch Day" event, such as room booking and AV setup, to ensure a seamless environment.
* I will also concentrate on enforcing the "Semester Transition Protocol" by ensuring all feedback ledgers and external communications are securely housed in the researchers' project files before the semester ends.

**7. Please provide feedback on what support you may need in your role.**
* *Note: Synthesized based on project parameters.*
* I need continued commitment from faculty to clearly define their academic boundaries, ensuring the "Scope Check" remains a reliable gateway for new partnerships.
* I also need alignment from the broader management class to strictly adhere to stepping back from direct communication with external partners to prevent disorganization.
